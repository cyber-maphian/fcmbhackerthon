Banks offer various types of accounts, including savings accounts, checking accounts, certificates of deposit (CDs), and money market accounts. Each serves a different purpose.

To open a bank account, you typically need to visit a local branch, provide identification, complete an application, and deposit an initial sum of money. Online accounts may have different requirements.

A savings account is designed for saving money and typically offers a higher interest rate. A checking account is for everyday transactions and often allows check writing and debit card use.
